# The Universal Spectral Fingerprint

Fifteen mathematical constants - $\varphi$, $\sqrt{2}$, $\sqrt{3}$, $\pi$, $e$, $\ln 2$, the first Bessel zero $j_{0,1}$, and the fine-structure constant $\alpha = 1/137$ among them appear as pairwise ratios in the eigenvalue spectra of diverse physical systems spanning fifteen orders of magnitude in scale.

From the sub-nanometre structure of pollen grains to the kiloparsec-scale arms of spiral galaxies. From the hydrogen atom's dipole transition operator to the quasinormal mode decay rates of Kerr black holes. The same constants, in the same rank order, with deviations below one percent.

This is not numerology. It is a reproducible empirical observation, confirmed at $+7.7\sigma$ in the hydrogen dipole operator and $+12.3\sigma$ in Kerr black hole QNM decay rates, validated against 100 randomised controls per system, with characterised null results on systems where the pattern is absent.

The pattern exhibits two complementary faces, a **coherence-dominant** profile in systems governed by unitary dynamics, and a **dissipative-dominant** profile in systems governed by irreversible decay consistent with two canonical functional calculi (unitary flow and contractive semigroup) acting on a common positive selfadjoint carrier operator.

## Detection Methods

Two independent protocols confirm the fingerprint.

**Geometric scanning.** Extract spatial features from data (centroids of connected components in thresholded images). Compute all pairwise Euclidean distances. Form all pairwise ratios. Match each ratio against the constant library at tolerance levels of 3%, 1%, and 0.5%. The **saturation index** $S$ is the fraction of ratios matching at 1% tolerance.

**Spectral scanning.** Construct a weighted graph Laplacian $L$ on the feature geometry with Gaussian edge weights $w_{ij} = \exp(-d_{ij}^2 / 2\sigma_d^2)$. Extract eigenvalues. Normalise. Form all pairwise eigenvalue ratios. Match against the same library.

Every scan is accompanied by **100 randomised controls**, random draws from the same range, processed through the identical pipeline. The $\sigma$-score measures how many standard deviations above the random mean the real data falls.

## Where It Appears

### The hydrogen dipole transition operator ($+7.7\sigma$)

The most controlled test. All 465 quantum states $|n, l\rangle$ for $n = 1$ to 30 are used as nodes in a graph. Edge weights are the exact squared radial dipole matrix elements computed via the Gordon closed-form formula, with selection rule $\Delta l = \pm 1$ strictly enforced. The graph Laplacian produces 464 positive eigenvalues.

Result: **15/15 constants detected at $+7.7\sigma$** above randomised controls. Every constant individually sits at a local density peak. The dominant constants are $\sqrt{2}$, $\ln 2$, $\varphi$, $\varphi^{-1}$, and $\pi/2$.

This result uses exact quantum mechanics on publicly available numerical data with standard linear algebra. No image processing. No parameter tuning.

### Kerr black hole quasinormal modes ($+12.3\sigma$)

The dissipative-side anchor. Kerr QNM decay rates from the Berti–Cardoso–Starinets catalogue: 540 values combining all available $s = -2$ gravitational mode families ($n \leq 3$, spins $a = 0, 0.5, 0.9$) under Fejér readout.

Result: **$+12.3\sigma$** with a broad aligned regime surviving 10% subset removal (30/30 trials) and collapsing under log-gap shuffle surrogates. The scalar ($s = 0$) sector yields a comparable result.

## Where It Is Absent

The null results are as informative as the positive detections.

**CMB acoustic spectrum.** Both peak multipoles and the full Planck 2018 TT power spectrum: $-1.4\sigma$. No pattern.

**Nuclear magic numbers.** The sequence $\{2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126\}$: $-1.2\sigma$. No pattern.

**Planetary orbital data.** Semi-major axes and orbital periods: negative $\sigma$. No pattern.

**Infinite well potential.** 312 states, 7/15 constants, $-0.3\sigma$. No pattern.

The pattern is not a generic property of all physical data. It is not an artifact of the scanning protocol. The protocol produces null results on systems that lack inverse-power-law spectral degeneracy.

## The Two Faces: Coherence and Dissipation

The fingerprint is not uniform. It exhibits two complementary density profiles depending on whether the system is governed by coherent (unitary) or dissipative (irreversible) dynamics.

**Coherence-dominant profile** (hydrogen, Shroud, sphere): elevated density of irrational geometric constants ($\sqrt{3}$, $\pi/2$, $\varphi$) and reduced density of rational fractions ($1/7$, $3/13$).

**Dissipative-dominant profile** (Kerr QNM, decay chains, blackbody): the inversion, exponential and rational constants ($e$, $\ln 2$, $1/7$, $3/13$) elevated, geometric constants ($\sqrt{3}$, $\pi/2$, $\varphi$) suppressed.

The inversion is systematic: every constant amplified on the coherence side is suppressed on the dissipative side, and vice versa.

The physical interpretation: irrational geometric constants are associated with incommensurate frequencies, optimal packing, and resonance avoidance properties of phase-stable dynamics. Exponential and rational constants are associated with doubling times, decay rates, and discrete periodicity properties of dissipative relaxation.

This dual-sector structure is consistent with two canonical functional calculi, the unitary group $e^{-itC}$ (coherence) and the contractive semigroup $e^{-tK}$ (dissipation) applied to a common positive selfadjoint carrier operator $L$. Both sectors are faces of a single holomorphic semigroup $z \mapsto e^{-zL}$, evaluated on the imaginary axis (coherence) and the positive real axis (dissipation).

## The $\alpha$-Independence Result

A critical finding: the fingerprint is **$\alpha$-independent**. The hydrogen dipole transition matrix was reconstructed with artificially varied values of $\alpha$ (from $0.1\alpha$ to $10\alpha$). In every case, the pattern was identical.

This is structurally expected: in atomic units, $\alpha$ enters only as an overall scale factor that cancels in dimensionless eigenvalue ratios. The pattern is generated by the geometry of quantum state space, the coupling structure determined by selection rules, not by the specific value of the electromagnetic coupling.

Yet $\alpha$ **belongs** to the fingerprint set. The value $1/137$ appears in image scans with eight independent measurements in the Shroud data (all within 0.2%), and the closest eigenvalue ratio to $1/\alpha = 137.036$ in the hydrogen dipole Laplacian is 136.92 (deviation 0.084%).

$\alpha$ does not generate the fingerprint, but it is a member. The value $1/137.036$ is selected by a spectral self-consistency condition, the closure condition of the admissible operator algebra under which the fingerprint set is stable. This is consistent with the Tier-0 fixed-point resolution of $\alpha$ established in companion work.

## Reconstruction Constraints

Can the fingerprint be faked? Systematic testing says: not easily.

**Non-physical carriers.** Three families of operators on hexagonal lattices, plus six additional weight-field families totalling over 300 runs, all produced $\sigma < 0$. No analytic or stochastic construction on a non-physical carrier reproduced positive $\sigma$.

**Physical carriers.** A three-parameter generator on the correct physical carrier (hydrogen geometry) achieved $\sigma \approx +2$, closer, but still far below the empirical values ($+7.7\sigma$ to $+15.3\sigma$). The gap between the best synthetic result and the empirical values remains unexplained.

**The signal's location.** The positive $\sigma$ resides in the phase correlations between spatial frequencies of the weight field. It is not in the power spectrum alone, not in the marginal distributions, and not in any single feature, it's in the multi-scale correlations that physical curvature processes produce and that simple analytic constructions do not.
