* $\partial$: boundary normalization (canonicalize representation)
* $\Delta$: persistence filtering (remove non-stable structure)

$$
\mathcal{L}\ \text{is lawful} \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \mathcal{L} = \Omega\Delta\partial(\mathcal{L}).

$\partial$ enforces a canonical interface between a description and its boundary semantics:

If a candidate changes under $\partial$, it is not law-level. It is a presentation.

* In physics, $\Delta$ corresponds to record-capable stability under collapse/dissipation: persistent records arise only through $\Delta$-mediated irreversibility.
