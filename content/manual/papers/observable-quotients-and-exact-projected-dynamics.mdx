## Reading notes

The dynamical layer. A bounded observation operator
$K: \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{src}} \to \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{obs}}$ always produces
an exact observable quotient $\mathcal{H}_K = \mathcal{H}_{\mathrm{src}}/\ker K$,
canonically bijective onto $\mathrm{Ran}\,K$, with bounded inverse in the
inherited norm exactly when the range is closed. The paper insists on keeping
four phenomena apart: **(D1)** exact loss ($\ker K \neq \{0\}$), **(D2)**
unstable inversion (nonclosed range), **(D3)** controlled low-rank
approximation ($\lVert K - K_r \rVert = \sigma_{r+1}$), and **(D4)** failure
of autonomous dynamics. Compactness supplies (D3) and by itself implies
neither (D1) nor (D2); (D4) implies (D1) but not conversely.

<Theorem title="Semigroup descent and autonomous closure">
An induced evolution on the observable state exists if and only if the source
semigroup preserves the readout kernel: $U(t)\ker K \subseteq \ker K$. For an
orthogonal resolved/unresolved splitting with bounded generator $L$,
autonomous first-order closure valid for <em>every</em> source initial state
is equivalent to $PLQ = 0$.
</Theorem>

When closure fails, the exact projected equation (a Mori–Zwanzig-type
identity, derived purely by variation of constants) carries two extra terms:
a deterministic unresolved-initial-state term $\eta_{z_0}(t)$. This term is
specified by the unresolved initial state rather than by a stochastic force.
The second contribution is the exact memory kernel
$\mathcal{K}(\tau) = PLQ\,e^{\tau QLQ}QLP$, a history operator rather than a
postulated friction coefficient. The derivation uses no ensemble, Markov, or timescale approximation
anywhere.

<Theorem title="Minimal dynamical completion">
The unresolved response map descends to an injective map on
$E_{\mathrm{dyn}} = \mathcal{H}_Q/N_Q$, the coarsest extension of the resolved
state determining every resolved response; every sufficient extension maps
onto it. In finite dimensions its size is the rank of a Kalman-type
observability matrix. Minimality is relative to the declared target;
directions in $N_Q$ remain part of the source even when that target cannot see them.
</Theorem>

The same architecture is carried into field theory: eliminating a quadratic
sector yields the exact Schur-complement effective operator $A - BC^{-1}B^*$
(possibly, but not automatically, nonlocal), and stationary elimination in a
diffeomorphism-invariant action yields a covariant effective stress-energy
defined as a genuine variational response, with conservation of the total
stress-energy and an explicit exchange current between sectors.

<ResultScope>
Paper 5 establishes exact operator statements parameterized by the observation
map $K$, generator $L$, and action. A specified physical model supplies those
objects and therefore determines the content of its memory and effective
corrections. The Schur theorem applies to bounded operators and classical
tree-level elimination; quantum effective actions require their own
construction. The paper is self-contained, with Papers 1–4 providing
conceptual context.
</ResultScope>
