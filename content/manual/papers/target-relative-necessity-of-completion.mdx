## Reading notes

A surjective, noninjective readout necessarily discards distinctions. Paper 2
determines exactly when those distinctions matter: the answer is a property of
the question asked, not of the readout alone. A **target** $Q$ is formalized as a correct-answer map
$a_Q: S \to A_Q$ on the reduced source.

<Theorem title="Exact target-solvability criterion">
$Q$ admits an exact readout-only solution if and only if $a_Q$ is constant on
every fiber of $p$. Consequently a lost source relation obstructs $Q$
precisely when it is <em>active</em> for $Q$, meaning that the realized answer varies
across some readout fiber. Globally inactive loss never obstructs: that is
what makes coarse descriptions viable, not defective.
</Theorem>

When exactness fails, no auxiliary quantity computed from the readout can
repair it (everything readout-computed is still fiber-constant). The honest
residual is the **compatible-answer set** $\mathsf A_Q(t) = a_Q(p^{-1}(t))$,
the unique pointwise-least uniformly sound set-valued rule. It is a set, not
a probability: a probabilistic prediction answers a different target and
requires extra input not contained in the readout.

<Theorem title="Necessary separation and the coarsest completion">
Every readout extension sufficient for $Q$ must separate every pair of source
states with different correct answers, and the joint target image
$E_Q = \mathrm{Im}(p, a_Q)$ is itself sufficient and universally coarsest:
every sufficient extension surjects uniquely onto it. This is the floor any
repair must contain. It retains target-relevant distinctions rather than reconstructing the source.
</Theorem>

The worked example is flat $U(1)$ connections on a circle. Curvature is a
deliberately restricted readout that collapses the whole flat moduli space
$S_{\mathrm{flat}} \cong U(1)$ to a point. This is perfectly adequate for the
flatness target, fatal for the charged-scalar spectral target. The spectrum
sees holonomy only up to inversion: the minimal spectral completion is the
inversion quotient coordinatized by $\cos\theta$, strictly coarser than the
full holonomy that oriented transport requires. One source, one readout, two
targets, two different minimal completions. The same criterion decides
finite-time dynamical closure: an autonomous readout propagator exists iff
the future readout is constant on present readout fibers.

<ResultScope>
Completion is target-relative. Fiber-constant targets remain exactly solvable
from a lossy readout, while active targets require the separation proved by the
universal property. The construction determines the least sufficient
information structure; operational access is a separate realization problem.
Here, completion means a readout extension by target-relevant distinctions,
distinct from metric completion and hidden-variable adjunction.
</ResultScope>
