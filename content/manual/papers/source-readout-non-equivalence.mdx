## Reading notes

The paper's central move is to keep three relations rigorously apart. Start
from a space $\Omega$ of admissible realization states, declare the physical
equivalence $\simeq_{\mathrm{phys}}$ (gauge, coordinates, relabelling), and
pass to the reduced source $S = \Omega/\!\simeq_{\mathrm{phys}}$ *before*
anything else. The invariant readout then induces a surjection $p: S \to T$.

- **Exact quotient presentation**: $T \cong S/\!\sim_p$ always holds. The
  readout labels the readout-equivalence classes without error or omission.
- **Representative selection**: a section of $p$ picks one state per fiber. A
  section is a convention, not an inverse.
- **Source equivalence**: $p$ bijective. Only in this degenerate case does the
  readout carry the whole reduced source.

<Theorem title="Fiber–descent theorem">
A physically invariant source relation $r: S \to V$ is a function of readout
data alone, meaning that it factors as $r = \bar r \circ p$, if and only if $r$ is constant
on every fiber of $p$. A relation that varies within a single fiber descends
through no post-processing of the readout whatsoever.
</Theorem>

<Theorem title="Equivariant-section criterion">
With a group $G$ acting on $S$ and $T$ and $p$ equivariant, a $G$-equivariant
section of $p$ exists if and only if every readout stabilizer $G_t$ fixes a
point of its fiber. A single fiber with empty stabilizer-fixed set excludes
every symmetry-compatible deterministic reconstruction rule.
</Theorem>

Two worked models realize the obstructions. In the finite marked-carrier
occupation model, the permutation group acts transitively on fibers, so no
equivariant selector exists. An equivariant *probability-valued* lift does
exist, but an ensemble is not a realized state. The Hopf fibration
$h: S^3 \to S^2$ realizes the obstruction continuously and also separates the
equivariant obstruction from the independent topological obstruction of a
nonvanishing first Chern class.

<ResultScope>
Exact quotient presentation is fully compatible with non-singleton fibers and
with failure of lawfulness or dynamical closure at the readout level.
Noninjectivity identifies multiplicity; a nominated relation or reconstruction
target determines which part of that multiplicity matters. Gauge redundancy is
removed before this analysis begins. Physical applications therefore specify
the source domain, physical equivalence, readout, and a non-descending relation
or reconstruction obstruction. The theorems then decide descent and
equivariant reconstruction exactly.
</ResultScope>
