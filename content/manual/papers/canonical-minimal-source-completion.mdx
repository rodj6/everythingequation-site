## Reading notes

Papers 1 and 2 posed the completion problem; this paper supplies the
constructive step. Given the reduced source $S$, the surjective readout
$p: S \to T$, and an externally *nominated* set-indexed family of eligible
(physically invariant) relations $\{r_\alpha: S \to V_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in A}$,
form the joint map $j_A = (p, (r_\alpha)_\alpha)$ and set

$$E_A \;=\; \mathrm{Im}\, j_A \;\cong\; S/\!\sim_A,$$

the quotient by agreement of the readout and every nominated relation.

<Theorem title="Canonical minimal completion (terminality)">
$E_A$ is terminal in the category of surjective relation-sufficient
extensions: every extension on which the readout and all nominated relations
are well defined admits a unique surjection onto $E_A$. This yields
existence, uniqueness up to unique isomorphism, and exact minimality — the
coarsest sufficient extension — without nominating a preferred representative
of any readout fiber.
</Theorem>

The supporting structure is worked out in full: an exact separation criterion
for redundant versus indispensable relation coordinates; canonical refinement
maps for nested families with a cocycle law; invariance under faithful
reparameterization of value spaces (the completion depends on distinctions
carried, not on labels); the completion determined directly by a family of
target-answer maps, with a canonical surjection from the relation completion
onto it (the target completion may be strictly coarser); the gauge-reduced
form (completion cannot resurrect redundancy already quotiented away); and
the topological form, distinguishing quotient from subspace topology.

The flat $U(1)$ circle example makes it concrete: oriented transport
completion returns the full holonomy circle $U(1)$, while the charged-scalar
spectral target needs only the strictly coarser inversion quotient
$U(1)/(z \sim z^{-1}) \cong [-1,1]$, with the canonical refinement
$e^{i\theta} \mapsto \cos\theta$ exhibiting the general relation-to-target
surjection.

<ClaimBoundary>
Three standing confusions are dispelled: minimality is not smallness;
completion is not reconstruction (they coincide only when the nominated
family separates points of the source); and coordinates are relative to the
nomination. The nomination of the relation family is the one load-bearing
external input the mathematics cannot supply. Nothing here establishes that
the source is empirically real, that lost distinctions ought to be restored,
or that $E_A$ is experimentally accessible or geometric — geometric
realization is Paper 4's subject.
</ClaimBoundary>
