## Reading notes

Paper 1 is a non-equivalence principle, not an obstruction machine. This
paper supplies the missing certificate: it fixes a public target, a frozen
readout state, and a lost realization role, and defines **essential closure
slots** generated from the target specification alone.

<Definition title="Certified active failure profile">
A certified verdict that a lost role $u$ is required by an essential slot $s$
and that no checked repair, discharge, or public surrogate clears that slot.
</Definition>

<Theorem title="Bridge principle (informal)">
Readout loss <em>plus</em> certified active slot failure blocks sound full
public closure. Neither alone suffices.
</Theorem>

The first-order instantiation distinguishes a checker-accepted derivation
from resolved public proof closure from the frozen base, and is explicitly
conditional on declared certified subcalculi.

<ClaimBoundary>
Certified proof failure forbids resolved closure from the frozen base. It
does not assert the target formula is false, globally unprovable, or that no
checker-accepted derivation of any kind exists. The paper emits a
completion-need record; it does not construct completions or prove
initiality.
</ClaimBoundary>
