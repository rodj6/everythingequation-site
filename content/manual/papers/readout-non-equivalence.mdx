## Reading notes

The paper's central move is to separate two relations that are usually
conflated. For a realization/readout map $\rho: \mathcal{C} \to S$:

- **Quotient presentation**: $S \cong \mathcal{C}/\sim_\rho$ — always holds
  when $S = \mathrm{Img}(\rho)$. The readout is *exact*.
- **Realization-structure equivalence**: $S \simeq_{\mathrm{real}} \mathcal{C}$
  — requires the readout domain to recover the full realization-relevant
  structure via admissible recovery maps.

<Theorem title="Domain-level non-equivalence (informal)">
If no admissible realization-faithful section of $\rho$ exists, then
$S \not\simeq_{\mathrm{real}} \mathcal{C}$ even though
$S \cong \mathcal{C}/\sim_\rho$ as a quotient.
</Theorem>

The statistical-mechanics instance is handled with care: the decisive
obstruction is not fiber multiplicity but the absence of an admissible
*invariant* package-role class in some macro-fiber under the
macro-indiscernibility symmetry group — proved via an equivariant no-selector
theorem and verified in a concrete finite occupation-number model.

<ClaimBoundary>
The results are confined to a formally defined class of ordinary
nondegenerate macro-only coarse-grained presentations. Faithful, enriched,
degenerate, representative-rigid, and ensemble-targeted presentations are
classified as explicit exceptions. The paper does not claim every
coarse-graining is non-faithful.
</ClaimBoundary>
