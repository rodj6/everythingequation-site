The present chapter defines the dark sector: the dark-energy and cosmological-constant branches, the vacuum-energy response, the dark-matter branch alternatives with their observational discriminators, the low-boundary cross-link, and the associated proof targets. The chapter receives the cosmological dark-sector interface from Chapter 12 and the parameter inputs from Chapter 11. It derives the relation between the equation of state and the branch evolution function from the continuity equation, states the cosmological-constant branch with $\rho_\Lambda=\Lambda/(8\pi G)$, types the vacuum projection map $\mathcal P_\Lambda^{grav}$ together with its quantitative suppression target, and splits the dark-matter density into primordial and induced components. It does not select a dark-sector branch and does not derive $\Lambda$, dark matter, dark energy, or the low-boundary source; in particular, the cosmological-constant problem remains open under the companion programme R6 of Chapter 17, with secondary open problems under R5 and R9; all remain active throughout, and nothing in this chapter discharges them.

## 13.3 Dark energy

For a separately conserved dark-energy component, $p_{\rm DE}=w_{\rm DE}(\bar a)\rho_{\rm DE}$, and the density evolves as $\rho_{\rm DE}(\bar a)=\rho_{{\rm DE},0}F_C(\bar a)$ with the branch evolution function $F_C$ derived from the continuity equation in (13.8)–(13.10) below.

A dynamical fluid branch must also specify

$$
\delta p_{\rm DE}^{\rm gi}
=c_{s,{\rm DE}}^2\delta\rho_{\rm DE}^{\rm gi}
+\delta p_{\rm nad}^{\rm gi},
$$

its anisotropic stress, exchange current, initial perturbations, and stability range. These constitutive data describe a physical branch only when they arise from a separately owned covariant action or covariant closed-time-path fluid functional. For example, an irrotational scalar-fluid branch may use

$$
\Gamma_{\rm DE}[g,\varphi]
=\int_M\sqrt{-g}\,P(X,\varphi)\,d^4x,
\qquad
X=-\frac12g^{\mu\nu}\nabla_\mu\varphi\nabla_\nu\varphi,
$$

$$
T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm DE}
=P_{,X}\nabla_\mu\varphi\nabla_\nu\varphi+Pg_{\mu\nu},
\qquad
p_{\rm DE}=P,
\qquad
\rho_{\rm DE}=2XP_{,X}-P.
$$

A more general fluid branch must provide its analogous covariant variational functional and derive its stress and exchange current from it. A prescribed $w_{\rm DE}$, sound speed, or anisotropic stress without such an owned functional is a phenomenological parametrization, not a physical dynamical-dark-energy branch of the theory. The constant-volume projection of any admitted dark-energy action is removed and included only in $\Lambda_{\rm br}$; its remaining dynamical stress appears exactly once in the gravitational and cosmological equations.

## 13.6 The Relation Between the Equation of State and the Branch Evolution Function

Start from the continuity equation for the closure branch:

$$
\boxed{
\dot\rho_C+3H(1+w_C)\rho_C=0.
}
\tag{13.8}
$$

Using $H=\dot a/a$, rewrite

$$
\frac{d\rho_C}{\rho_C}
=
-3(1+w_C(a))\,d\ln a.
$$

Integrating from $a=1$ to $a$,

$$
\boxed{
F_C(a)
=
\exp
\left[
-3
\int_1^a
(1+w_C(a'))\,d\ln a'
\right].
}
\tag{13.9}
$$

For constant $w_C$,

$$
\boxed{
F_C(a)=a^{-3(1+w_C)}.
}
\tag{13.10}
$$

Equations (13.8)–(13.10) are the continuity-derived branch relations of this chapter.

The cosmological-constant branch is defined by

$$
\boxed{
w_C=-1.
}
\tag{13.11}
$$

Substituting $w_C=-1$ into (13.10) gives

$$
\boxed{
F_C(a)=1,
}
\tag{13.12}
$$

and therefore

$$
\boxed{
\rho_C(a)=\rho_{C0}.
}
\tag{13.13}
$$

The cosmological constant and the effective vacuum density are related by

$$
\boxed{
\rho_\Lambda=\frac{\Lambda}{8\pi G}.
}
\tag{13.14}
$$

Thus the cosmological-constant term enters the Friedmann equation through

$$
\boxed{
\frac{\Lambda}{3}
=
\frac{8\pi G}{3}\rho_\Lambda.
}
\tag{13.15}
$$

The magnitude and source of $\Lambda$ are not fixed by (13.14); they belong to the cosmological-constant projection question of this chapter’s proof targets and to $R6$.

## 13.8 Vacuum-Energy Projection and Ownership

Let $\mathcal P_{\rm vac}$ be the preregistered renormalization projector onto the constant unit-operator functional $\int\sqrt{-g}\,d^4x$. It separates the constant vacuum term in the same action split as the gravitational sector (Chapter 7):

$$
S_M^{\rm cl}+\Gamma_{\rm QFT}^{\rm ren}
=
-\int d^4x\sqrt{-g}\,\rho_{\rm vac}^{\rm const}
+S_{M,\rm dyn}^{\rm cl}
+\Gamma_{\rm QFT,dyn}^{\rm ren},
\tag{13.16}
$$

where both dynamic remainders lie in $\ker\mathcal P_{\rm vac}$. The constant contribution is assigned once to the branch variable $\Lambda_{\rm br}$. Classical stress, dynamic renormalized QFT stress, and dark-energy terms exclude the constant already assigned. A constant-shift response is claimed only for the declared HT/global branch and not for arbitrary spacetime-dependent vacuum contributions.

<a id="139-ordinary-and-fixed-flux-gravitational-branches"></a>

## 13.9 Ordinary and Fixed-Flux Gravitational Branches

Let $\mathfrak b_G\in\{\mathrm{Ord},\mathrm{HT}\}$. These are alternative off-shell actions. The ordinary branch is

$$
S_G^{\rm Ord}[g]
=\frac1{16\pi G_R}\int_M(R-2\Lambda_{\rm br})\,\mathrm{vol}_g.
$$

The Henneaux–Teitelboim branch is

$$
S_G^{\rm HT}[g,\lambda,A_3]
=\frac1{16\pi G_R}\int_MR\,\mathrm{vol}_g
-\frac1{8\pi G_R}\int_M\lambda(\mathrm{vol}_g-dA_3).
$$

Variation with respect to $A_3$ gives $d\lambda=0$, while variation with respect to $\lambda$ gives $\mathrm{vol}_g=dA_3$, subject to fixed-flux boundary data. On shell the integration constant is denoted $\lambda_{\rm HT}$; after the constant matter-vacuum term is included, the gravitational combination is $\Lambda_{\rm br}=\lambda_{\rm HT}+8\pi G_R\rho_{\rm vac}^{\rm const}$. The ordinary and HT actions are never summed.

On a fixed-flux HT branch, a constant shift of matter vacuum energy may be absorbed into the integration constant,

$$
(\rho_{\rm vac}^{\rm const},\lambda_{\rm HT})
\sim
(\rho_{\rm vac}^{\rm const}+c,
\lambda_{\rm HT}-8\pi G_Rc),
$$

leaving $\Lambda_{\rm br}=\lambda_{\rm HT}+8\pi G_R\rho_{\rm vac}^{\rm const}$ invariant within that fixed-flux sector. This statement does not prove that $\Lambda_{\rm br}$ is small, stable under all local radiative corrections, or equal to the observed value.

### 13.9.1 HT fixed-flux construction

Fix a finite oriented cut-region family

$$
\mathfrak U_{\rm cut}
=
\{U_\alpha\}_{\alpha=1}^{N_{\rm cut}},
\tag{13.17}
$$

three-form fields $\mathcal A_{3,\alpha}^{\rm HT}$, four-form strengths $F_{4,\alpha}=d\mathcal A_{3,\alpha}^{\rm HT}$, and independently varied multipliers $\sigma_\alpha$. Use

$$
S_{\rm HT}
=
\sum_\alpha
\left[
-\int_{U_\alpha}\sigma_\alpha\sqrt{-g}\,d^4x
+
\int_{U_\alpha}\sigma_\alpha F_{4,\alpha}
\right],
\tag{13.18}
$$

with fixed-flux boundary condition

$$
\left.
\delta\mathcal A_{3,\alpha}^{\rm HT}
\right|_{B_{\rm flux}}=0.
\tag{13.19}
$$

Independent variation gives

$$
F_{4,\alpha}
=
\sqrt{-g}\,d^4x
\quad\text{on }U_\alpha,
\tag{13.20}
$$

and

$$
d\sigma_\alpha=0
\quad\text{on each connected }U_\alpha.
\tag{13.21}
$$

The flux-volume relation is

$$
\int_{U_\alpha}F_{4,\alpha}
=
\operatorname{Vol}_g(U_\alpha).
\tag{13.22}
$$

Its claim is limited: under the declared global fixed-flux boundary conditions, a constant matter-vacuum shift is transferred to the integration-constant branch. This does not prove radiative stability for arbitrary local effective operators.

The covariant fixed-flux branch is the Henneaux–Teitelboim formulation \[[36](/monograph/bibliography#ref-HenneauxTeitelboim1989)\] with the boundary-term interpretation of \[[37](/monograph/bibliography#ref-BuchmullerDragon2022)\]; manifestly local vacuum-energy sequestering \[[38](/monograph/bibliography#ref-KaloperPadillaStefanyszynZahariade2016)\] is a distinct, stronger mechanism and is cited only as contrast. No claim is made that this branch predicts the observed value of the cosmological constant.

$$
\boxed{
\epsilon_\Lambda\ll1.

## 13.11 Dark-Matter Branch Split

The dark-matter branch is split as

$$
\boxed{
\rho_{DM}(a)=\rho_{prim}(a)+\rho_{ind}(a).
}
\tag{13.26}
$$

The primordial branch $\rho_{prim}$ is defined operationally by the following conditions.

1.  Existence: it exists independently of late-time nonlinear structure formation.

2.  Large-scale behavior: it behaves as nonrelativistic matter at large scale unless a branch states otherwise.

3.  Friedmann role: it contributes to $\Omega_{DM}$.

4.  Microphysics status: unresolved.

5.  Open under R6.

The induced branch $\rho_{ind}$ is defined operationally by the following conditions.

1.  Emergence: it emerges from effective geometry, record-dependency, modified clustering, or projection-induced stress.

2.  Environmental dependence: it may correlate with baryonic, structural, curvature, or record-geometric environments.

3.  Gravitational role: it contributes gravitationally like dark matter in selected regimes.

4.  Discriminator requirement: it requires operational discriminators from $\rho_{prim}$.

5.  Open under R6.

This chapter does not select either branch.

Record stress, dark-sector stress, vacuum terms, and gravitational EFT corrections are singly owned: the action-ownership assignment gives each term exactly one owner, and no component may be counted in more than one branch of the cosmological accounting (Chapters 7, 12, and 16).

## 13.15 Dark-Matter Branches

A kinetic dark-matter branch lives on the future mass shell $P_m^+=\{(x,p):g_{\mu\nu}p^\mu p^\nu=-m^2,\ p^0>0\}$ and supplies the covariant phase-space equation

$$
\mathcal L_{\rm ps}f_{\rm DM}
:=
p^\mu\frac{\partial f_{\rm DM}}{\partial x^\mu}
-\Gamma^i_{\alpha\beta}p^\alpha p^\beta
\frac{\partial f_{\rm DM}}{\partial p^i}
+F_{\rm DM}^{\,i}\frac{\partial f_{\rm DM}}{\partial p^i}
=C_{\rm DM}[f]+S_{\rm DM},
$$

$$
T_{\rm DM}^{\mu\nu}=\int dP\,p^\mu p^\nu f_{\rm DM},
$$

where $dP$ is the invariant mass-shell measure and $F_{\rm DM}^{\,i}$ is any admitted nongeodesic force tangent to $P_m^+$ (zero for collisionless geodesic dark matter). An effective-fluid branch supplies $\rho_{\rm DM}$, $p_{\rm DM}$, sound speed, anisotropic stress, exchange current, and a range of validity as a moment closure of an owned kinetic or covariant action description. Its provenance may be written

$$
\rho_{\rm DM}=\rho_{\rm prim}+\rho_{\rm ind},
$$

without assuming that both terms are nonzero; the operational definitions of the two branches are those of the branch-split section above.

The pure primordial and pure induced branches are exclusive. A hybrid branch is admissible only when the two contributions arise from disjoint action or kinetic terms and their exchange current is specified, so that the same effective stress is not counted twice.

The two possibilities differ observationally through the discriminators tabulated earlier in this chapter; the CMB, clustering, lensing, phase-space, and structure-growth discriminators are outputs of the Chapter 12 transfer system.
