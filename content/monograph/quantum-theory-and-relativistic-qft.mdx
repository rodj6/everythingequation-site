This chapter defines the quantum projection channel of the theory. It expands the typed channel map introduced in Chapter 3, using the notation and standards fixed in Chapter 4. For each record-capable realization support object $Y\in\mathsf{Real}_0$, the quantum channel returns a structured quantum readout object. The channel formalizes Hilbert-space state structure, the observable algebra, effects, POVMs, Born probability measures, dynamics, tensor-product composition, instruments, and record preforms, together with the locally covariant relativistic QFT structure on which the later sectors depend. The source-side derivation of the quantum readout, the source origin of Born weights, and the single-record realization selector remain active proof obligations of the companion programme.

The chapter supplies quantum-side structures to Chapter 6 for record deposition and objectivity, and to Chapter 8 for the quantum-to-geometry bridge. It does not define record deposition mechanics or the geometry bridge.

## 5.1 The Quantum Channel Map

The quantum projection channel expands the typed channel map

$$
\boxed{
\Pi_Q:\mathsf{Real}_0\to\mathcal{O}_Q
}
\tag{5.1}
$$

introduced in Chapter 3. For each record-capable realization support object $Y\in\mathsf{Real}_0$, the quantum channel returns a structured quantum readout object

$$
\boxed{
Q_Y=\Pi_Q(Y)\in\mathcal{O}_Q.
}
\tag{5.2}
$$

## 5.2 Quantum Theory and Locally Covariant QFT

<a id="521-quantum-readout"></a>

### 5.2.1 Quantum readout

On a prepared realization $Y$, a quantum readout consists of a Hilbert space $\mathcal H_Y$, a normal state space, an observable algebra, a measurable outcome space and its effects. In the type-I reduction the state space is

$$
\mathcal S(\mathcal H_Y)
=
\{\rho\in\mathcal T_1(\mathcal H_Y):\rho\ge0,\ \operatorname{Tr}\rho=1\}.
\tag{5.3}
$$

For a POVM $E:\Sigma\to\mathcal B(\mathcal H_Y)$,

$$
E(B)\ge0,
\qquad E(\Omega)=I,
\qquad
E\!\left(\bigcup_nB_n\right)=\sum_nE(B_n)
\tag{5.4}
$$

in the weak operator topology for disjoint $B_n$. The Born measure is

$$
\boxed{
\mu_\rho(B)=\operatorname{Tr}[\rho E(B)].
}
\tag{5.5}
$$

The trace pairing with a positive trace-class state makes (5.5) a normalized countably additive probability measure. It assigns weights to alternatives; it does not by itself select or deposit a record.

Closed dynamics is generated by a self-adjoint Hamiltonian,

$$
U(t)=e^{-it\mathsf H},
\qquad
\rho(t)=U(t)\rho(0)U(t)^\dagger,
\tag{5.6}
$$

while open dynamics is completely positive and trace preserving,

$$
\Phi(\rho)=\sum_aK_a\rho K_a^\dagger,
\qquad
\sum_aK_a^\dagger K_a=I.
\tag{5.7}
$$

For a composite nonrelativistic system,

$$
\mathcal H_{AB}=\mathcal H_A\otimes\mathcal H_B,
\qquad
\rho_{AB}\in\mathcal S(\mathcal H_{AB}),
$$

without any assumption that $\rho_{AB}$ factorizes. In relativistic QFT, composition is instead expressed by the net of local algebras and their causal commutation relations; a tensor-product factorization is not assumed across arbitrary local regions.

For general local QFT the algebraic formulation is primary. If $\mathcal A$ is a von Neumann algebra with predual $\mathcal A_*$, a Schrödinger-picture instrument is a countably additive map

$$
\mathcal I:\Sigma\longrightarrow\operatorname{CP}(\mathcal A_*),
\tag{5.8}
$$

whose nonselective operation preserves normalization. Its effect is $E(B)=\mathcal I(B)^*(I)$, and the probability is $p_\omega(B)=\omega(E(B))$. Equation (5.5) is recovered in a density-operator representation.

<a id="522-locally-covariant-gauge-qft"></a>

### 5.2.2 Locally covariant gauge QFT

Let $\mathsf{Loc}_{\rm spin}$ be the category of oriented, time-oriented, globally hyperbolic spin spacetimes with admissible gauge-bundle data and causality-preserving embeddings. A locally covariant QFT is a functor

$$
\mathcal A:\mathsf{Loc}_{\rm spin}\longrightarrow\mathsf{Alg}_*,
\tag{5.9}
$$

obeying covariance, Einstein causality and the time-slice property. Gauge symmetry is treated by the BV/BRST complex. For a background $\mathfrak b$, the perturbative physical algebra is

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal A_{\rm phys}^{\rm form}(\mathfrak b)
=
H^0\!\left(\widehat s_{\rm BV},
\mathcal A_{\rm int}^{\rm ren}(\mathfrak b)[[\hbar,\mathbf g]]\right).
}
\tag{5.10}
$$

The quantum master equation and the relevant local, global and mixed anomaly classes must vanish or be cancelled on an admitted branch. Renormalization is locally covariant and carries an explicit prescription and scale $\mu$; changes of scheme are related by the renormalization group and threshold matching.

At the classical level the BV action and antibracket obey

$$
(S_{\rm BV},S_{\rm BV})=0,
\qquad
s_{\rm BV}=(S_{\rm BV},\,\cdot\,),
\qquad
s_{\rm BV}^{2}=0.
$$

Gauge fixing is a choice of Lagrangian submanifold, equivalently a gauge-fixing fermion where that description applies; physical observables are degree-zero cohomology classes and do not depend on that choice.

For fermionic matter the background contains a tetrad $e^a{}_{\mu}$, a spin connection $\omega_\mu{}^{ab}$ and a spin structure compatible with the gauge bundle. The covariant derivative is

$$
D_\mu
=
\partial_\mu
+\frac14\omega_\mu{}^{ab}\gamma_{ab}
-i\sum_Ag_AA_\mu^AT_A,
\tag{5.11}
$$

with the appropriate representation matrices $T_A$. The renormalized BV effective action $\Gamma_{\rm QFT}^{\rm ren}[g,e,A,\Phi;\mu]$ satisfies the quantum master equation up to the declared anomaly class. On a physical branch,

$$
\frac12(\Gamma,\Gamma)-i\hbar\Delta\Gamma=0,
\tag{5.12}
$$

or the nonzero right-hand side is cancelled by the stated matter content and counterterms. The field equations are the stationary conditions $\delta\Gamma_{\rm QFT}^{\rm ren}/\delta\Phi=0$, interpreted perturbatively where appropriate.

Admitted states are positive normalized algebraic states satisfying the Hadamard or corresponding microlocal spectrum condition required to define the renormalized stress tensor. For spacelike separated regions $O_1$ and $O_2$, Einstein causality gives

$$
[\mathcal A(O_1),\mathcal A(O_2)]=0.
\tag{5.13}
$$

Renormalized time-ordered products obey locality, covariance, scaling and the Ward identities. Changing the scale $\mu$ is governed by the renormalization group, and integrating through a mass threshold requires matching of effective couplings and operators. These conditions state the QFT structure that a successful realization must recover; they are not placed in the source signature.

On an anomaly-free physical branch, perturbative unitarity holds on BRST cohomology order by order. A trace anomaly need not vanish; in four dimensions it has the renormalized form

$$
\langle T^\mu{}_{\mu}\rangle
=
\sum_i\beta_i\mathcal O_i
+aE_4+cW_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}W^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma}
+b\Box R,
$$

up to the chosen operator basis and scheme-dependent total-derivative terms. This contribution belongs to the same renormalized effective action and stress tensor used below; it is not added a second time in the gravitational equation.

Equation (5.10) is a formal $*$-algebra and is not automatically a $C^*$- or von Neumann algebra. In particular, there is no presumed $*$-homomorphism from the whole formal power-series algebra into a $C^*$-algebra. An operational completion in a relatively compact detector region $\mathfrak D$ is instead the datum

$$
\mathfrak O_{\mathfrak D}
=
\bigl(
\mathcal B_{\mathfrak D},
\mathcal B^0_{\mathfrak D},
\mathcal D^{\rm res}_{\mathfrak D},
\operatorname{Res}_{\mathfrak D},
\operatorname{Asym}_{\mathfrak D}
\bigr).
\tag{5.14}
$$

Here $\mathcal B_{\mathfrak D}$ is a unital $C^*$-algebra, $\mathcal B^0_{\mathfrak D}$ is a norm-dense $*$-subalgebra, and $\mathcal D^{\rm res}_{\mathfrak D}$ is a $*$-algebra of coupling-dependent observables for which the chosen Borel–Écalle, analytic or nonperturbative reconstruction is defined. The partial resummation

$$
\operatorname{Res}_{\mathfrak D}:
\mathcal D^{\rm res}_{\mathfrak D}/\mathcal N_{\mathfrak D}
\longrightarrow
\mathcal B^0_{\mathfrak D}
\tag{5.15}
$$

is a $*$-isomorphism onto $\mathcal B^0_{\mathfrak D}$, with $\mathcal N_{\mathfrak D}$ the declared BRST- and detector-null ideal. The asymptotic map $\operatorname{Asym}_{\mathfrak D}$ sends elements of $\mathcal B^0_{\mathfrak D}$ to their formal BV/BRST expansions and satisfies

$$
\operatorname{Asym}_{\mathfrak D}
\circ\operatorname{Res}_{\mathfrak D}(F)
\sim F
\quad(F\in\mathcal D^{\rm res}_{\mathfrak D})
\tag{5.16}
$$

to the declared asymptotic order, while respecting local covariance, the time-slice property and the physical cohomology. A genuinely nonperturbative construction may specify $\mathcal B_{\mathfrak D}$ directly, but it must still supply (5.16) on a dense comparison domain. Existence, covariance and uniqueness of $\mathfrak O_{\mathfrak D}$ for an interacting four-dimensional branch are companion-theorem obligations, not consequences of the formal algebra.

Only after such an operational completion has been established may a positive normalized state $\bar\omega$ on $\mathcal B_{\mathfrak D}$ be used in the GNS construction:

$$
(\pi_{\bar\omega},\mathcal H_{\bar\omega},\Omega_{\bar\omega})
=\operatorname{GNS}(\mathcal B_{\mathfrak D},\bar\omega),
\qquad
\mathcal N_{\mathfrak D,\bar\omega}
=\pi_{\bar\omega}(\mathcal B_{\mathfrak D})''.
\tag{5.17}
$$

Normal instruments act on $\mathcal N_{\mathfrak D,\bar\omega}$, never directly on (5.10):

$$
\mathcal I_{\mathfrak D}:\Sigma_{\mathfrak D}
\longrightarrow\operatorname{CP}_{\rm n}(\mathcal N_{\mathfrak D,\bar\omega}),
\qquad
p_{\bar\omega}(B)
=\widetilde\omega\bigl(\mathcal I_{\mathfrak D,B}(1)\bigr).
\tag{5.18}
$$

For pairwise disjoint $B_n$ they satisfy

$$
\mathcal I_{\mathfrak D,\cup_nB_n}(A)
=
\sum_n\mathcal I_{\mathfrak D,B_n}(A)
\quad\text{ultraweakly},
\qquad
\mathcal I_{\mathfrak D,\Omega_{\mathfrak D}}(1)=1.
\tag{5.19}
$$

For an admissible embedding $\chi:\mathfrak D\hookrightarrow\mathfrak D'$, covariance requires

$$
\alpha_\chi\circ\mathcal I_{\mathfrak D,B}
=
\mathcal I_{\mathfrak D',\chi_*B}\circ\alpha_\chi.
\tag{5.20}
$$

For sequential instruments,

$$
p(B_1,\ldots,B_n)
=
\widetilde\omega\!\left(
\mathcal I^{(1)}_{B_1}\circ\cdots\circ
\mathcal I^{(n)}_{B_n}(1)\right).
\tag{5.21}
$$

Spacelike local algebras commute. If a nonselective operation in $\mathfrak D_A$ acts identically on $\mathcal N_{\mathfrak D_B}$ for spacelike $\mathfrak D_B$, then the unconditioned $B$ marginal is independent of the choice at $A$. Selective conditioning may alter correlations but not enable signalling.

<a id="523-state-dependent-stress-tensor-and-the-record-interface"></a>

### 5.2.3 State-dependent stress tensor and the record interface

Backreaction is governed by the in-in, or closed-time-path, effective action \[[70](/monograph/bibliography#ref-Schwinger1961), [71](/monograph/bibliography#ref-Keldysh1965)\] rather than by a state-independent in-out functional. For a Hadamard initial state $\omega$, let

$$
\Gamma_{\rm QFT,CTP}^{\rm ren}
[g^+,\Phi^+;g^-,\Phi^-\mid\omega]
=
\Gamma_{\rm QFT,dyn}^{\rm ren}
[g^+,\Phi^+;g^-,\Phi^-\mid\omega]
+\Gamma_{\rm grav,loc}^{\rm ren}[g^+]
-\Gamma_{\rm grav,loc}^{\rm ren}[g^-]+C_\omega .
\tag{5.22}
$$

The dynamical influence functional is normalized at a fixed reference configuration by

$$
\Gamma_{\rm QFT,dyn}^{\rm ren}
[g_{\rm ref},\Phi_{\rm ref};g_{\rm ref},\Phi_{\rm ref}\mid\omega]=0.
\tag{5.23}
$$

The subtraction changes no functional derivative. The local pure-metric counterterms $\Gamma_{\rm grav,loc}^{\rm ren}$ renormalize the gravitational couplings and are included once, on the gravitational side of (7.54); they are excluded from the dynamical QFT stress. The state-dependent renormalized stress is

$$
\boxed{
\langle T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm QFT,dyn}\rangle_\omega
=
-\frac{2}{\sqrt{-g}}
\left.
\frac{\delta\Gamma_{\rm QFT,dyn}^{\rm ren}}
{\delta g^{+\mu\nu}}
\right|_{g^+=g^-=g,\,\Phi^+=\Phi^-=\Phi} .
}
\tag{5.24}
$$

On backgrounds with external fields $b_A$, its Ward identity has the form

$$
\nabla^\mu\langle T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm QFT,dyn}\rangle_\omega
=
\sum_A\frac{1}{\sqrt{-g}}
\left.
\frac{\delta\Gamma_{\rm QFT,dyn}^{\rm ren}}{\delta b_A^+}
\right|_{+=-}
\nabla_\nu b_A
+\mathcal A_\nu^{\rm diff},
\tag{5.25}
$$

where the diffeomorphism anomaly term must vanish or be cancelled for ordinary covariant coupling to gravity.

For a finite-resolution detector cell $B$ with positive measure, or for an outcome in a standard-Borel outcome space on which a regular conditional instrument is defined almost everywhere, the QFT instrument supplies a record preform

$$
D_B^{\rm pre}
=
(B,\mathfrak D,\mathcal I_B,w_B,\widetilde\omega_B,
\text{context and realization lineage}).
\tag{5.26}
$$

Here $w_B=\mu(B)$ for a finite-resolution cell and is the registered Radon–Nikodym density at $B$ for an almost-everywhere regular conditional instrument. In a continuous POVM a singleton may have zero measure without being physically excluded: its conditional object is understood through the regular conditional instrument on its almost-everywhere domain or through a registered finite-resolution cell. Only events contained in a measurable null set on which no such conditional instrument is defined are excluded from actionable record preparation. Crucially, the instrument produces alternatives and their preforms. It neither selects an outcome nor deposits an objective record.

The geometry $g$ entering (5.9)–(5.25) is itself reconstructed and dynamically constrained below. Conversely, (5.24) enters the gravitational equation. The QFT algebra, its operational completion, the instruments, the objective archive and the gravitational solution must therefore belong to one simultaneous branch:

$$
\bigl(g,\Phi,\omega,\mathcal I,u,
\mathcal A^{\rm obj},\mathcal M_{\rm dim},\mathfrak m_R\bigr)
\in\mathcal S_{\rm QFT-R-G}(Y,\mathcal N,B),
\tag{5.27}
$$

where the set on the right is defined explicitly by (8.10)–(8.11). Its existence and stability are part of the R3/R9 programme, not assumed by writing the sector equations separately.

## 5.3 Hilbert Space and Quantum State Space

<a id="531-definition-53---hilbert-space"></a>

### 5.3.1 Definition 5.3 — Hilbert Space

For each $Y\in\mathsf{Real}_0$, $\mathcal{H}_Y$ is a complex separable Hilbert space with inner product

$$
\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_Y.
$$

Unless a later finite-dimensional specialization is declared, $\mathcal{H}_Y$ is allowed to be infinite-dimensional and separable. Let

$$
\mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}_Y)
$$

denote the bounded operators on $\mathcal{H}_Y$, and

$$
\mathcal{T}_1(\mathcal{H}_Y)
$$

denote the trace-class operators on $\mathcal{H}_Y$.

<a id="532-definition-54---state-space"></a>

### 5.3.2 Definition 5.4 — State Space

The quantum state space is

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{H}_Y)
=
\left\{
\rho\in\mathcal{T}_1(\mathcal{H}_Y):
\rho\ge0,\ \mathrm{Tr}(\rho)=1
\right\}.
}
\tag{5.28}
$$

A pure state is the special case

$$
\boxed{
\rho=|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|,
\qquad
\|\psi\|_Y=1.
}
\tag{5.29}
$$

The conditions in (5.28) define Tier-1 quantum readout structure. Their source derivation is routed through the companion programmes R1 and R2 of Chapter 17.

<a id="54-observable-algebra-effects-and-povms"></a>

## 5.4 Observable Algebra, Effects, and POVMs

<a id="541-definition-55---observable-algebra"></a>

### 5.4.1 Definition 5.5 — Observable Algebra

The observable algebra $\mathcal{A}_{obs,Y}$ is taken to be a von Neumann algebra

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal{A}_{obs,Y}\subseteq\mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}_Y).
}
\tag{5.30}
$$

This convention fixes the operator setting of the present chapter. Later sector chapters may specialize to finite-dimensional matrix algebras or to concrete field-theoretic algebras, but the general channel convention is von Neumann-algebraic.

<a id="542-definition-56---effects"></a>

### 5.4.2 Definition 5.6 — Effects

An effect is an operator $E\in\mathcal{A}_{obs,Y}$ satisfying

$$
\boxed{
0\le E\le I.
}
\tag{5.31}
$$

The order means that $E$ is positive and $I-E$ is positive.

<a id="543-definition-57---povm"></a>

### 5.4.3 Definition 5.7 — POVM

Let $(\Omega_Y,\Sigma_Y)$ be a measurable outcome space. A POVM is a map

$$
\boxed{
E_Y:\Sigma_Y\to\mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}_Y)
}
\tag{5.32}
$$

such that

$$
\boxed{
E_Y(B)\ge0
\quad\text{for all }B\in\Sigma_Y,
}
\tag{5.33}
$$

$$
\boxed{
E_Y(\Omega_Y)=I,
}
\tag{5.34}
$$

and $E_Y$ is countably additive in the weak operator topology:

$$
\boxed{
\left\langle\psi,E_Y\!\left(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}B_n\right)\phi\right\rangle
=
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}
\langle\psi,E_Y(B_n)\phi\rangle
}
\tag{5.35}
$$

for pairwise disjoint $B_n\in\Sigma_Y$ and all $\psi,\phi\in\mathcal{H}_Y$.

For a discrete outcome set,

$$
\boxed{
E_i=E_Y(\{i\}),
\qquad
E_i\ge0,
\qquad
\sum_iE_i=I.
}
\tag{5.36}
$$

The set of all POVMs satisfying these conditions is denoted

$$
\boxed{
\mathsf{POVM}_Y.
}
\tag{5.37}
$$

POVM effects may be taken in $\mathcal A_{obs,Y}$ when the measurement is internal to the observable algebra; otherwise the POVM is an allowed bounded-operator measurement interface in $\mathcal B(\mathcal H_Y)$.

<a id="55-born-measure"></a>

## 5.5 Born Measure

Given $\rho\in\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{H}_Y)$ and $E_Y\in\mathsf{POVM}_Y$, define

$$
\boxed{
\mu_{\rho,Y}(B)=\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E_Y(B)),
\qquad B\in\Sigma_Y.
}
\tag{5.38}
$$

For a discrete outcome $i$,

$$
\boxed{
p_i=\mu_{\rho,Y}(\{i\})=\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E_i).
}
\tag{5.39}
$$

The Born measure supplies probability weights over measurement alternatives. It does not select a single realized deposited record. The selector variable $\chi_i$ is reserved for Chapter 6 and for the selection-depth programme R7 of Chapter 17.

<a id="56-elementary-probability-lemmas"></a>

## 5.6 Elementary Probability Lemmas

<a id="561-lemma-51---state-effect-pairing"></a>

### 5.6.1 Lemma 5.1 — State-Effect Pairing

For any state $\rho\in\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{H}_Y)$ and any effect $E$ with $0\le E\le I$,

$$
\boxed{
0\le\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E)\le1.
}
\tag{5.40}
$$

**Proof.** Since $\rho\ge0$ is trace-class and $E\ge0$ is bounded, $\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E)\ge0$. Since $E\le I$, the operator $I-E\ge0$. Therefore

$$
\mathrm{Tr}(\rho(I-E))\ge0.
$$

Using linearity of trace,

$$
\mathrm{Tr}(\rho)-\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E)\ge0.
$$

Because $\mathrm{Tr}(\rho)=1$, it follows that

$$
\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E)\le1.
$$

Thus $0\le\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E)\le1$. $\square$

<a id="562-lemma-52---born-measure-is-a-probability-measure"></a>

### 5.6.2 Lemma 5.2 — Born Measure Is a Probability Measure

If $\rho\in\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{H}_Y)$ and $E_Y$ is a POVM on $(\Omega_Y,\Sigma_Y)$, then

$$
\boxed{
\mu_{\rho,Y}(B)=\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E_Y(B))
}
\tag{5.41}
$$

defines a probability measure on $(\Omega_Y,\Sigma_Y)$.

**Proof.** *Non-negativity.* For every $B\in\Sigma_Y$, $E_Y(B)\ge0$. By Lemma 5.1 applied to $E_Y(B)$, $\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E_Y(B))\ge0$. Hence $\mu_{\rho,Y}(B)\ge0$.

*Normalization.* Since $E_Y(\Omega_Y)=I$,

$$
\mu_{\rho,Y}(\Omega_Y)
=
\mathrm{Tr}(\rho I)
=
\mathrm{Tr}(\rho)
=
1.
$$

*Countable additivity.* Let $\{B_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$ be pairwise disjoint measurable sets. By weak operator countable additivity of $E_Y$,

$$
E_Y\!\left(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}B_n\right)
=
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}E_Y(B_n)
$$

in the weak operator sense. Since $\rho$ is trace-class, the trace pairing with bounded operators is normal, so it preserves countable increasing sums of positive operators. Therefore

$$
\mu_{\rho,Y}\!\left(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}B_n\right)
=
\mathrm{Tr}\!\left(\rho E_Y\!\left(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}B_n\right)\right)
=
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\mathrm{Tr}(\rho E_Y(B_n))
=
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\mu_{\rho,Y}(B_n).
$$

Thus $\mu_{\rho,Y}$ is a normalized, nonnegative, countably additive measure, hence a probability measure. $\square$

<a id="57-quantum-dynamics"></a>

## 5.7 Quantum Dynamics

<a id="571-definition-58---closed-dynamics"></a>

### 5.7.1 Definition 5.8 — Closed Dynamics

For closed Tier-1 quantum readout dynamics, let $\mathsf H_Y$ be a self-adjoint Hamiltonian on $\mathcal{H}_Y$. The sans-serif symbol $\mathsf H_Y$ is the Chapter 4 Hamiltonian convention, distinguishing the Hamiltonian from the Higgs doublet $H$ (Chapter 9) and the Hubble readout $H(a)$ (Chapter 12). The unitary evolution is

$$
\boxed{
U_Y(t)=e^{-it\mathsf H_Y}
}
\tag{5.42}
$$

in units $c=\hbar=1$. The state evolves as

$$
\boxed{
\rho(t)=U_Y(t)\rho(0)U_Y(t)^\dagger.
}
\tag{5.43}
$$

When $\hbar$ is restored,

$$
U_Y(t)=e^{-it\mathsf H_Y/\hbar}.
$$

<a id="572-definition-59---open-dynamics"></a>

### 5.7.2 Definition 5.9 — Open Dynamics

An open quantum dynamics map is a completely positive trace-preserving map

$$
\boxed{
\Phi:\mathcal{T}_1(\mathcal{H}_Y)\to\mathcal{T}_1(\mathcal{H}_Y).
}
\tag{5.44}
$$

A Kraus representation has the form

$$
\boxed{
\Phi(\rho)=\sum_a K_a\rho K_a^\dagger,
\qquad
\sum_aK_a^\dagger K_a=I.
}
\tag{5.45}
$$

The map $\Phi$ is part of Tier-1 quantum-readout structure. The source derivation of the admissible dynamics class is routed through the companion programmes R1 and R2 of Chapter 17.

<a id="58-tensor-product-composition"></a>

## 5.8 Tensor-Product Composition

For a bipartite quantum readout system,

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal{H}_{AB}=\mathcal{H}_A\otimes\mathcal{H}_B.
}
\tag{5.46}
$$

A product state has the form

$$
\boxed{
\rho_{AB}=\rho_A\otimes\rho_B.
}
\tag{5.47}
$$

The state space also allows entangled states, i.e. states $\rho_{AB}\in\mathcal{S}(\mathcal{H}_{AB})$ not expressible as convex combinations of product states.

The marginal state on $A$ is obtained by partial trace:

$$
\boxed{
\rho_A=\mathrm{Tr}_B(\rho_{AB}).
}
\tag{5.48}
$$

This composition block is required by Chapter 6 for decoherence and objectivity, and by Chapter 8 for the quantum-record-geometry bridge constructions.

<a id="59-measurement-contexts-and-instruments"></a>

## 5.9 Measurement Contexts and Instruments

Let $\mathcal A$ be a Tier-1 von Neumann algebra and let $\mathcal A_*$ be its normal predual. A normal state is a positive normalized element $\omega\in\mathcal A_*$. In a Hilbert-space representation with density operator $\rho$, one has $\omega(A)=\operatorname{Tr}(\rho A)$.

A complete measurement context is

$$
\boxed{
C_{\rm meas}
=
(\Omega,\Sigma,E,\mathcal I,o_C).
}
$$

Here $(\Omega,\Sigma)$ is a standard Borel outcome space, $E:\Sigma\to\mathcal A$ is a POVM, $\mathcal I$ is a normal quantum instrument, and $o_C$ is a measurable outcome code. The code is part of the physical/source context. It is fixed before the source selector coordinate is evaluated, transforms under context isomorphisms, and never depends on the outcome subsequently selected.

In Schrödinger form, the instrument is

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal I:\Sigma\to \operatorname{CP}(\mathcal A_*).
}
$$

For every $B\in\Sigma$, $\mathcal I(B)$ is completely positive and trace-nonincreasing. For pairwise disjoint $B_n$,

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal I\!\left(\bigcup_{n=1}^{\infty}B_n\right)(\omega)
=
\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\mathcal I(B_n)(\omega)
}
$$

with convergence in the predual norm. The nonselective map is normalization preserving:

$$
\boxed{
\mathcal I(\Omega)(\omega)(I)=\omega(I)=1.
}
$$

The associated POVM is obtained from the Heisenberg dual:

$$
\boxed{
E(B)=\mathcal I(B)^*(I),
\qquad
p_\omega(B)=\omega(E(B)).
}
$$

In the density-operator representation this reduces to

$$
p_\rho(B)=\operatorname{Tr}[\rho E(B)].
$$

This algebraic form is primary for relativistic QFT. The trace formula is the normal-representation and ordinary-QM limit.

Instruments and operational probabilities follow the operational approach of \[[19](/monograph/bibliography#ref-DaviesLewis1970)\]; continuous-outcome measuring processes follow \[[20](/monograph/bibliography#ref-Ozawa1984)\].

<a id="510-record-preforms-and-candidate-deposition"></a>

## 5.10 Record Preforms and Candidate Deposition

For a discrete outcome $i$, the quantum-side record preform is

$$
\boxed{
R_i=(E_i,p_i,\sigma_i,\lambda_i^{\rm lab}),
\qquad
p_i=\omega(E_i).
