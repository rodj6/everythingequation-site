## 12.1 Role and Scope

Chapter 12 develops the cosmological readout of the theory: the FLRW background, gauge-invariant perturbations, thermal history, and the routes to CMB, large-scale-structure, lensing and gravitational-wave observables, all on the solved gravitational branch of Chapters 7â€“8.

Cosmology is the large-scale Tier-1 readout of a compatible geometry, matter/QFT state, any record-exclusive contribution, parameter branch, and initial-data class. ## 12.2 Effective field equation and exact-once accounting

The cosmological branch inherits

$$
G_{\mu\nu}[g]+\Lambda_{\rm br}g_{\mu\nu}
+\mathcal Q_{\mu\nu}^{\rm grav}
=8\pi G_R\left(
T_{\mu\nu}^{M,\rm dyn}
+T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm QFT,dyn}
+T_{\mu\nu}^{R,\rm tot}
+\chi_{\rm DM}T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm DM}
+\chi_{\rm DE}T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm DE}
\right).
$$

The matter and quantum terms are the variations of the disjoint $S_M^{\rm dyn}$ and state-dependent $\Gamma_{\rm QFT,dyn}^{\rm ren}$ defined in Chapter 9. If a species is treated by the quantum functional, it is absent from $S_M^{\rm dyn}$. Pure-metric local terms from QFT renormalization occur only through $\Lambda_{\rm br}$, $G_R$, and $\Gamma_{\rm high}^{\rm grav}$. The record functional is the matched remainder after subtracting every term already represented in $S_M^{\rm dyn}$ or $\Gamma_{\rm QFT,dyn}^{\rm ren}$, with the EFT matching datum $\mathfrak m_R$ fixed before $\operatorname{Sol}_G(\cdot;\mathfrak m_R)$ is evaluated. Archive kinematics does not automatically supply an additional stress: if the record variables merely reparameterize already owned degrees of freedom, then $\Gamma_{R,\rm CTP}=0$ and $T_{\mu\nu}^{R}=0$. Here $\chi_{\rm DM},\chi_{\rm DE}\in\{0,1\}$. A switch is one only when its separately owned covariant action or kinetic functional contains degrees of freedom absent from both the matter and QFT terms, and is zero otherwise. Thus $T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm DM}$ and $T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm DE}$ are action-derived branch stresses rather than additional phenomenological copies.

Here

$$
T_{\mu\nu}^{R,\rm tot}
:=T_{\mu\nu}^{R}+\chi_{MR}T_{\mu\nu}^{MR},
\qquad \chi_{MR}\in\{0,1\},
$$

where $T_{\mu\nu}^{R}$ is only the record-exclusive matched remainder. The interaction switch $\chi_{MR}$ is one only for a separately owned matterâ€“record interaction absent from the matter, QFT and record-exclusive functionals; otherwise it is zero. Likewise $\chi_{\rm DE}=1$ only on a separately owned dynamical dark-energy branch and is zero on a pure-$\Lambda$ branch. The tensor $\mathcal Q_{\mu\nu}^{\rm grav}$ is obtained by varying the pure-metric higher-curvature action $\Gamma_{\rm high}^{\rm grav}$; it is not also inserted as an independent fluid or QFT stress.

Covariance gives

$$
\nabla^\mu T_{\mu\nu}^{(A)}=J_\nu^{(A)},
\qquad
\sum_AJ_\nu^{(A)}=0,
$$

together with the interior Noether identity $\nabla^\mu\mathcal Q_{\mu\nu}^{\rm grav}=0$, with boundary fluxes cancelled by the boundary completion of $\Gamma_{\rm high}^{\rm grav}$. Every dynamical contribution appears exactly once in this equation and once in its perturbation.

## 12.5 Continuity Equation and Scaling Laws

For the declared branch-owned components $A$, including the record and correction accounts, the component continuity equations with exchange are

$$
\dot\rho_A+3H(\rho_A+p_A)=Q_A,
\qquad
\sum_AQ_A=0,
\tag{12.11}
$$

the FLRW form of the covariant exchange identity of the effective field equation; no additional record or correction exchange term is counted beside the component sum. For each independently conserved perfect-fluid component,

$$
\boxed{
\dot\rho_i+3H(\rho_i+p_i)=0.
}
\tag{12.12}
$$

More generally, for an independently conserved component with equation of state $w_I(\bar a)=p_I/\rho_I$,

$$
\rho_I(\bar a)=\rho_{I0}F_I(\bar a),
\qquad
F_I(\bar a)
=
\exp\left[-3\int_1^{\bar a}
\left(1+w_I(\bar a')\right)d\ln\bar a'\right].
\tag{12.13}
$$

Using $p_i=w_i\rho_i$ with constant $w_i$,

$$
\dot\rho_i+3H(1+w_i)\rho_i=0.
$$

Since $H=\dot a/a$,

$$
\frac{d\rho_i}{\rho_i}
=
-3(1+w_i)\frac{da}{a}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\boxed{
\rho_i(a)=\rho_{i0}a^{-3(1+w_i)}.
}
\tag{12.14}
$$

The standard scalings are:

$$
\boxed{

(w_m=0),

\tag{12.15}
$$

$$
\boxed{

\qquad
(w_r=1/3),

\tag{12.16}
$$

$$
\boxed{
\rho_\Lambda(a)=\rho_{\Lambda0}
\qquad
(w_\Lambda=-1).
}
\tag{12.17}
$$

The curvature contribution to $E^2$ is the additive term $\Omega_{k0}\bar a^{-2}$, while the instantaneous fractional curvature density is $\Omega_k(\bar a)$ of (12.2); the two are distinct objects in the dimensionless Friedmann readout.

## 12.6 Scalar, vector, and tensor perturbations

Use conformal time $d\eta=dt/\bar a$ and the covariant derivative $D_i$ of the reference constant-curvature spatial metric $\gamma_{ij}$, for which ${}^{(3)}R[\gamma]=6\mathcal K_{\rm FLRW}$. Decompose

$$
\begin{aligned}
ds^2=\bar a^2(\eta)\{&-(1+2\phi)d\eta^2
+2(D_iB+S_i)d\eta\,dx^i\\
&+[(1-2\psi)\gamma_{ij}+2D_iD_jE
+2D_{(i}F_{j)}+h_{ij}]dx^idx^j\},
\end{aligned}
$$

with

$$
D^iS_i=D^iF_i=0,
\qquad D^ih_{ij}=0,
\qquad \gamma^{ij}h_{ij}=0.
$$

Under $\eta\mapsto\eta+T$ and $x^i\mapsto x^i+D^iL+L^i$, with $D_iL^i=0$, define $\sigma=B-E'$. The gauge-invariant Bardeen potentials \[[48](/monograph/bibliography#ref-Bardeen1980), [49](/monograph/bibliography#ref-KodamaSasaki1984), [50](/monograph/bibliography#ref-MaBertschinger1995)\] and vector shear are

$$
\Phi=\phi+\mathcal H\sigma+\sigma',
\qquad
\Psi=\psi-\mathcal H\sigma,
\qquad
V_i=S_i-F_i',
\qquad \mathcal H=\frac{\bar a'}{\bar a}.
$$

For each species $A$,

$$
\delta\rho_A^{\rm gi}=\delta\rho_A+\bar\rho_A'\sigma,
\qquad
\delta p_A^{\rm gi}=\delta p_A+\bar p_A'\sigma,
$$

and the momentum and anisotropic stress split into scalar, transverse-vector, and transverse-traceless parts. The master perturbation equation is

$$
\delta G_{\mu\nu}
+\Lambda_{\rm br}\delta g_{\mu\nu}
+\delta\mathcal Q_{\mu\nu}^{\rm grav}
=8\pi G_R\delta T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm owned},
$$

where $\delta T_{\mu\nu}^{\rm owned}$ contains each classical, renormalized-QFT, record-exclusive, separately owned interaction, dark-matter, and dynamical-dark-energy contribution exactly once. Scalar, vector, and tensor equations are obtained by the orthogonal SVT projectors on the chosen harmonic domain.

In the spatially flat, uncorrected and noninteracting limit, introduce the scalar velocity potential by $\delta T^0{}_i=(\bar\rho+\bar p)D_iv^{\rm gi}$. The scalar Einstein equations are

$$
D^2\Psi-3\mathcal H(\Psi'+\mathcal H\Phi)
=4\pi G_R\bar a^2\delta\rho^{\rm gi},
$$

$$
\Psi'+\mathcal H\Phi
=-4\pi G_R\bar a^2(\bar\rho+\bar p)v^{\rm gi},
$$

$$
\Psi''+\mathcal H(\Phi'+2\Psi')
+(2\mathcal H'+\mathcal H^2)\Phi
+\frac13D^2(\Phi-\Psi)
=4\pi G_R\bar a^2\delta p^{\rm gi},
$$

$$
\left(D_iD_j-\frac13\gamma_{ij}D^2\right)(\Phi-\Psi)
=8\pi G_R\bar a^2\Pi^{S}_{ij}.
$$

For illustration, a separately conserved barotropic species with constant $w_A=c_{s,A}^2$ obeys

$$
\delta_A'+(1+w_A)(\vartheta_A-3\Psi')=0,
$$

$$
\vartheta_A'+\mathcal H(1-3w_A)\vartheta_A
+D^2\!\left(\Phi+\frac{c_{s,A}^2}{1+w_A}\delta_A\right)
-D^2\sigma_A=0,
$$

where $\delta_A=\delta\rho_A^{\rm gi}/\bar\rho_A$, $\vartheta_A$ is the scalar velocity divergence, and $\sigma_A$ is the scalar anisotropic-stress potential. Time-dependent equations of state, entropy pressure, massive kinetic species, exchange currents, and EFT/record corrections are governed by the kinetic equation below and by the perturbation of the same covariant conservation law; they are not represented by adding independent duplicate sources.

For transverse momentum $q_i^V$ and vector anisotropic-stress potential $\Pi_i^V$, defined so that its stress contribution is $D_{(i}\Pi^V_{j)}$, the vector constraint and propagation equations take the convention-fixed form

$$
(D^2+2\mathcal K_{\rm FLRW})V_i
=-16\pi G_R\bar a^2q_i^V+S_i^{V,\rm grav},
$$

$$
(\bar a^2V_i)'
=16\pi G_R\bar a^4\Pi_i^V+\bar a^2\widetilde S_i^{V,\rm grav}.
$$

Consequently, an unsourced perfect-fluid vector shear decays according to

$$
(\bar a^2V_i)'=0,
\qquad V_i\propto\bar a^{-2}.
$$

Any sustained vector mode must therefore identify a transverse momentum, anisotropic stress, topological source, or modified-gravity source in the field equation.

For tensor harmonics satisfying $-D^2Q_{ij}^{(\lambda)}=k_{\rm com}^2Q_{ij}^{(\lambda)}$, write $h_{ij}=\sum_\lambda h_\lambda Q_{ij}^{(\lambda)}$. Then

$$
h_\lambda''+2\mathcal Hh_\lambda'
+(k_{\rm com}^2+2\mathcal K_{\rm FLRW})h_\lambda
=16\pi G_R\bar a^2\Pi_\lambda^{\rm tot}
+S_\lambda^{\rm grav}.
$$

The curvature symbol $\mathcal K_{\rm FLRW}$ is used consistently here; $k_{\rm com}$ is the perturbation wavenumber.

## 12.9 Light-cone and propagation object

The observation map is evaluated on the solved geometry, not on a background coordinate distance by fiat. For a radial null ray,

$$

$$

\begin{cases}

$$
