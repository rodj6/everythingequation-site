# The Source-to-Readout Construction

## The Tier-0.5 recursive aperture

### 1. Role of the aperture

The aperture is the constructive transduction from an aperture-ready
realization seed to the maximal typed emission supported by a declared
family of Tier-1 readouts: a minimal full carrier when the analytic
conditions close, otherwise an effective response, a precise residue
or no carrier. It is neither a passive boundary nor a list of
acceptance tests.

For each aperture face \(a\), it is a partial functor

\[
\mathcal A_{1/2}^{a}:
\mathsf{Seed}_{1/2}
\dashrightarrow
\mathsf{Emit}_{1/2}^{a}.
\tag{A3.1}
\]

Its input is

\[
\mathcal Y=
(\mathcal H^{\rm pre},q^{\rm pre},K^{\rm pre},
\mathcal I_\Omega,\mathsf{Trace},
\operatorname{Cert}_0,\operatorname{Reg}^{\rm pre}),
\qquad K^{\rm pre}\ge0,
\tag{A3.2}
\]

and its full-carrier summand is

\[
\mathfrak S^a=
(\mathcal H^a,q^a,K^a,E^a,\nu^a,\mathcal W^a,J^a,
\tau^a,\mathcal R^a,h^a,\gamma_a,
\mathsf{CarrierRoute}^a).
\tag{A3.3}
\]

The residue \(\mathcal R^a\) is structure that affects the face but is
not represented by the regular spectral sector alone.  The symbol
\(h^a\), damping coefficient \(\gamma_a\) and carrier route are
selected before the field layer; they are not free Tier-1 additions.
The effective, residue and null summands are typed in Section 21.

The complete aperture factorization is

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal A_{1/2}^{a}
=
\operatorname{Spine}^{a}
\circ\operatorname{NoEx}^{a}
\circ\operatorname{Comp}^{a}
\circ\operatorname{Vis}^{a}
\circ\operatorname{Red}
\circ\operatorname{CRI}.
}
\tag{A3.4}
\]

The ordering in (A3.4) is fixed. Realizations are constructed first. Declared redundancy is reduced before physical relations are completed. Visibility is then formed. Missing but necessary relations are minimally restored. Only after these operations is no-excess reduction legitimate.

### 2. Canonical realization interface

For an admitted source \(X\) and prepared realization \(Y=J_{\rm real}(X,m)\), the canonical realization interface is

\[
\operatorname{CRI}(Y)=
\bigl(
\mathsf{Def}(Y),\mathsf{Real}(Y),
\operatorname{SeedPrep}_\Omega(Y),
\mathcal I_\Omega(X),\mathsf{Trace}_Y
\bigr).
\tag{A3.5}
\]

\(\mathsf{Def}(Y)\) is the class of boundary- and lineage-preserving realization deformations. \(\mathsf{Real}(Y)\) is the family of structures generated by those deformations. The interface is natural under strong source maps:

\[
\operatorname{CRI}(f_Y):
\operatorname{CRI}(Y)\longrightarrow
\operatorname{CRI}(Y').
\tag{A3.6}
\]

No Tier-1 field is selected by CRI. It supplies the realization family, its pre-carrier and its inherited invariants.

### 3. Reduction before visibility

Let \(\mathcal G_Y\) be the groupoid of transformations declared redundant on the realization: support relabellings, basis changes, internal frame changes, and any gauge action already defined at this stage. The reduced realization is

\[
\mathsf S_Y^{\rm red}
=\mathsf{Real}(Y)/\mathcal G_Y.
\tag{A3.7}
\]

At Hilbert-space level, let \(\mathcal N_{\rm red}\subset\mathcal H^{\rm pre}\) be the closed subspace generated by null gauge and redescription directions. Put

\[
\mathcal H^{\rm red}
=\mathcal H^{\rm pre}\ominus\mathcal N_{\rm red},
\qquad
P_{\rm red}:\mathcal H^{\rm pre}\to\mathcal H^{\rm red}.
\tag{A3.8}
\]

The pre-carrier descends precisely when the redundant subspace is reducing:

\[
K^{\rm pre}\mathcal N_{\rm red}
\subseteq\mathcal N_{\rm red},
\qquad
K^{\rm pre}(\mathcal N_{\rm red})^\perp
\subseteq(\mathcal N_{\rm red})^\perp.
\tag{A3.9}
\]

Because \(K^{\rm pre}\) is selfadjoint, either inclusion in (A3.9) implies the other. The reduced carrier is then

\[
K^{\rm red}
=K^{\rm pre}|_{\mathcal H^{\rm red}}.
\tag{A3.10}
\]

If (A3.9) fails, the alleged gauge direction changes physical evolution and is not redundant for that carrier. It must be retained or the carrier must be modified by a gauge-compatible constraint construction.

### 5. Probe and visibility operator

For \(b\in\mathcal B_a\), let

\[
V_b:\mathcal H^{\rm red}\to\mathcal V_b
\tag{A3.14}
\]

be a bounded probe. Choose positive probe weights \(\omega_b\) satisfying

\[
\sum_{b\in\mathcal B_a}\omega_b\|V_b\|^2<\infty.
\tag{A3.15}
\]

The face analysis operator is

\[
\boxed{
\mathbb V_a:
\mathcal H^{\rm red}\to
\bigoplus_{b\in\mathcal B_a}\mathcal V_b,
\qquad
\mathbb V_a\psi=
(\sqrt{\omega_b}V_b\psi)_{b\in\mathcal B_a}.
}
\tag{A3.16}
\]

Its positive visibility operator is

\[
\mathbb G_a=\mathbb V_a^*\mathbb V_a
=\sum_{b\in\mathcal B_a}\omega_bV_b^*V_b.
\tag{A3.17}
\]

The initially visible and silent subspaces are

\[
\mathcal H_{P,a}
=\overline{\operatorname{Ran}\mathbb V_a^*}
=(\ker\mathbb V_a)^\perp,
\qquad
\mathcal H_{Q,a}
=\ker\mathbb V_a.
\tag{A3.18}
\]

Let \(P_a\) and \(Q_a=I-P_a\) be the corresponding orthogonal projections. The visibility decomposition is

\[
\mathcal H^{\rm red}
=\mathcal H_{P,a}\oplus\mathcal H_{Q,a}.
\tag{A3.19}
\]

This decomposition is probe-relative. It does not yet license removal of \(\mathcal H_{Q,a}\), because a mode that is instantaneously silent can influence later visible evolution.

### 6. Exact observational quotient

On the reduced realization space, define

\[
s\sim_a s'
\quad\Longleftrightarrow\quad
V_b(s)=V_b(s')
\quad\text{for all }b\in\mathcal B_a.
\tag{A3.20}
\]

The exact observable quotient is

\[
\mathsf T_a
=\mathsf S_Y^{\rm red}/\!\sim_a
\cong\operatorname{Im}\mathbb V_a.
\tag{A3.21}
\]

The quotient is exact as a representation of probe-equivalence classes. It need not contain every relation required for the face dynamics or target equations.

### 7. Minimal relation completion

Let

\[
r_\alpha^a:
\mathsf S_Y^{\rm red}\to\mathcal R_\alpha^a,
\qquad \alpha\in A_a,
\tag{A3.22}

be the eligible relations required by the face compatibility equations \(\mathcal C_a\). Eligibility means invariance under \(\mathcal G_Y\).

Put

\[
R_a(s)=\bigl(r_\alpha^a(s)\bigr)_{\alpha\in A_a}.
\tag{A3.23}
\]

The completed face state is the joint image

\[
\boxed{
\mathsf E_a
=\operatorname{Im}(\mathbb V_a,R_a)
\subseteq
\mathsf T_a\times
\prod_{\alpha\in A_a}\mathcal R_\alpha^a.
}
\tag{A3.24}
\]

Equivalently,

\[
\mathsf E_a
\cong
\mathsf S_Y^{\rm red}/\!\sim_{A_a},
\tag{A3.25}
\]

where

\[
s\sim_{A_a}s'
\Longleftrightarrow
\left[
s\sim_a s'
\ \text{and}\
r_\alpha^a(s)=r_\alpha^a(s')
\ \forall\alpha\in A_a
\right].
\tag{A3.26}
\]

#### Theorem A3.1 — canonical face completion

\(\mathsf E_a\) is the unique coarsest extension of \(\mathsf T_a\) on which every relation \(r_\alpha^a\) descends.

**Proof.** Let \(E\) be any extension through which \(\mathbb V_a\) and every \(r_\alpha^a\) factor. The coordinate map from \(E\) to \(\mathsf T_a\times\prod_\alpha\mathcal R_\alpha^a\) has image in (A3.24), is surjective onto that image, and is uniquely determined by the factorization. Hence every sufficient extension maps uniquely and surjectively to \(\mathsf E_a\). \(\square\)

Completion restores only distinctions forced by the nominated face relations. It does not reconstruct the full source.

### 8. Dynamical visibility

Let the pre-carrier generate dissipative evolution

\[
\dot\Psi(t)=-K^{\rm red}\Psi(t).
\tag{A3.27}
\]

For the block calculation in this section, assume either that
\(K^{\rm red}\) is bounded, or more generally that
\(P_aD(K^{\rm red})\subseteq D(K^{\rm red})\), the diagonal blocks
generate contraction semigroups, and the off-diagonal block extends to
a bounded operator from \(\mathcal H_{Q,a}\) to
\(\mathcal H_{P,a}\). These conditions hold automatically for every
finite aperture seed.

Relative to (A3.19), write

\[
K^{\rm red}
=
\begin{pmatrix}
A_a&B_a\\
B_a^*&D_a
\end{pmatrix},
\tag{A3.28}
\]

where

\[
A_a=P_aK^{\rm red}P_a,\quad
B_a=P_aK^{\rm red}Q_a,\quad
D_a=Q_aK^{\rm red}Q_a.
\tag{A3.29}
\]

For \(x=P_a\Psi\) and \(z=Q_a\Psi\),

\[
\dot x=-A_ax-B_az,
\qquad
\dot z=-B_a^*x-D_az.
\tag{A3.30}
\]

Eliminating \(z\) gives the exact resolved equation

\[
\boxed{
\dot x(t)
=-A_ax(t)
-B_ae^{-tD_a}z_0
+\int_0^t
B_ae^{-(t-s)D_a}B_a^*x(s)\,ds.
}
\tag{A3.31}
\]

The second term carries unresolved initial data. The third is the exact memory term. The visible sector is autonomous for all initial states exactly when

\[
\boxed{B_a=P_aK^{\rm red}Q_a=0.}
\tag{A3.32}
\]

Thus instantaneous silence and dynamical irrelevance are different conditions.

### 9. Minimal dynamical residue

Define the unresolved response operator

\[
\mathbb O_a:
\mathcal H_{Q,a}\to
C([0,\infty),\mathcal H_{P,a}),
\qquad
(\mathbb O_az)(t)=B_ae^{-tD_a}z.
\tag{A3.33}
\]

The dynamically invisible subspace is

\[
\mathcal N_a^{\rm dyn}
=\ker\mathbb O_a
=\bigcap_{t\ge0}
\ker(B_ae^{-tD_a}).
\tag{A3.34}
\]

Because \(D_a\) is selfadjoint, \(\mathcal N_a^{\rm dyn}\) is closed and reducing for \(D_a\). The minimal dynamical residue is

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal E_a^{\rm dyn}
=\mathcal H_{Q,a}/\mathcal N_a^{\rm dyn}
\cong
(\mathcal N_a^{\rm dyn})^\perp.
}
\tag{A3.35}
\]

Two unresolved states define the same class in (A3.35) exactly when they generate the same visible response for all \(t\ge0\).

In finite dimension \(m=\dim\mathcal H_{Q,a}\),

\[
\mathcal N_a^{\rm dyn}
=\bigcap_{k=0}^{m-1}
\ker(B_aD_a^k),
\tag{A3.36}
\]

and

\[
\dim\mathcal E_a^{\rm dyn}
=\operatorname{rank}
\begin{pmatrix}
B_a\\B_aD_a\\ \vdots\\ B_aD_a^{m-1}
\end{pmatrix}.
\tag{A3.37}
\]

Equations (A3.35)–(A3.37) calculate, rather than guess, how much of the silent sector the aperture must retain.

### 10. Joint spectral visibility

At the no-excess stage, let \(\mathcal F_a^{\rm pre}\) be the declared
countable family of Borel response symbols already fixed before the
final symbol problem.  It contains a point-separating dissipative or
resolvent family:

\[
\mathcal F_a^{\rm pre}\supseteq
\{e^{-t\lambda}:t\in\mathbb T_a\}
\cup\{f_T(\lambda):T\in\mathbb U_a\}
\cup\{(\lambda+i)^{-1}\},
\tag{A3.38}
\]

where \(\mathbb T_a,\mathbb U_a\subset(0,\infty)\) are fixed
countable dense sets. The resolvent multiplier is itself injective on
\([0,\infty)\), so the generated Borel algebra separates the carrier
spectrum.

Let the response algebra generated by these symbols be

\[
\mathfrak A_a
=W^*(f(K^{\rm red}):f\in\mathcal F_a^{\rm pre}).
\tag{A3.38a}
\]

The coherent functions \(e^{-ith^a(\lambda)}\) and
\(\operatorname{sinc}(Th^a(\lambda)/2)\) are added only after
\(h^a\) has been selected by (A3.64c).  They therefore cannot alter the
space on which their own symbol was selected.

Define the response-cyclic space and fully invisible subspace by

\[
\mathcal H_{\rm cyc}^a
=\overline{\mathfrak A_a\mathcal H_{P,a}},
\qquad
\boxed{
\mathcal N_a
=(\mathcal H_{\rm cyc}^a)^\perp
=\left\{z\in\mathcal H_{Q,a}:
P_aAz=0\ \text{for every }A\in\mathfrak A_a\right\}.
}
\tag{A3.39}
\]

If \(\mathcal F_a^{\rm pre}\) separates points of
\(\operatorname{spec}(K^{\rm red})\), then (A3.39) removes exactly the
unresolved spectral multiplicity that no declared pre-symbol face
response can couple into the resolved sector.

The no-excess Hilbert space is

\[
\boxed{
\mathcal H_{\rm noex}^{a}
=\mathcal H_{\rm cyc}^a
=\mathcal H_{P,a}\oplus
(\mathcal H_{Q,a}\ominus\mathcal N_a).
}
\tag{A3.40}
\]

Let \(P_{\rm noex}^a\) be its orthogonal projection.  If the response
symbols generate \(W^*(K^{\rm red})\) on their joint spectral support,
then \(\mathcal N_a\) and \(\mathcal H_{\rm noex}^a\) reduce
\(K^{\rm red}\).  The no-excess carrier is then

\[
K_{\rm noex}^{a}
=K^{\rm red}|_{\mathcal H_{\rm noex}^{a}}.
\tag{A3.41}
\]

It remains positive and selfadjoint.

#### Theorem A3.2 — no-excess minimality

\(\mathcal H_{\rm noex}^{a}\) is the smallest reducing subspace containing \(\mathcal H_{P,a}\) and invariant under the response algebra \(\mathfrak A_a\).

**Proof.** The cyclic subspace

\[
\overline{\mathfrak A_a\mathcal H_{P,a}}
\tag{A3.43}
\]

is invariant under \(\mathfrak A_a\) and its adjoint algebra, hence is
reducing for \(\mathfrak A_a\).  Its orthogonal complement consists
precisely of \(z\in\mathcal H_{Q,a}\) whose matrix elements into
\(\mathcal H_{P,a}\) vanish for every \(A\in\mathfrak A_a\), which is
(A3.39).  Because \(\mathcal H_{P,a}\subseteq\mathcal H_{\rm cyc}^a\),
orthogonal decomposition by \(P_a\) gives (A3.40). Every reducing
subspace containing \(\mathcal H_{P,a}\) must contain its
\(\mathfrak A_a\)-cyclic closure. \(\square\)

This theorem gives the exact meaning of “no excess.”

### 11. Relation coordinates as carrier channels

For each relation \(r_\alpha^a\) that admits a Hilbert realization, let

\[
W_\alpha^a:
\mathcal H_{\rm noex}^{a}\to\mathcal Z_\alpha^a
\tag{A3.44}
\]

be its closed analysis map. Define the relation form

\[
q_{\rm rel}^a[\psi]
=\sum_{\alpha\in A_a}
\eta_\alpha\|W_\alpha^a\psi\|^2,
\qquad
\eta_\alpha>0.
\tag{A3.45}
\]

Assume the joint analysis map
\(\psi\mapsto(\sqrt{\eta_\alpha}W_\alpha^a\psi)_\alpha\)
is closed and densely defined. Then \(q_{\rm rel}^a\) is a closed
nonnegative form. Require the boundary form to be closed and
nonnegative and either require their common form domain to be complete
in the sum norm or impose the sufficient relative bound

\[
q_{\rm rel}^a[\psi]+q_{\rm bnd}^a[\psi]
\le c_a\,q_{\rm noex}^a[\psi]
+d_a\|\psi\|^2,
\qquad c_a,d_a<\infty,
\qquad
D(q_{\rm noex}^a)\subseteq
D(q_{\rm rel}^a)\cap D(q_{\rm bnd}^a).
\tag{A3.46}
\]

In the sum-norm alternative set
\(D(q^a)=D(q_{\rm noex}^a)\cap D(q_{\rm rel}^a)\cap
D(q_{\rm bnd}^a)\); in the relative-bound alternative set
\(D(q^a)=D(q_{\rm noex}^a)\).  Then the completed aperture form

\[
\boxed{
q^a[\psi]
=q_{\rm noex}^a[\psi]
+q_{\rm rel}^a[\psi]
+q_{\rm bnd}^a[\psi]
}
\tag{A3.47}
\]

is densely defined, closed and nonnegative. The bound \(c_a<1\)
would be required for a signed perturbation; no such restriction is
needed for the nonnegative relation and boundary forms used here.

By the representation theorem, there is a unique positive selfadjoint carrier

\[
\boxed{
K^a=\operatorname{Friedrichs}(q^a).
}
\tag{A3.48}
\]

This is the exact carrier-realization law:

\[
(\mathcal Y,a)
\xmapsto{\ \operatorname{Red,Vis,Comp,NoEx}\ }
q^a
\xmapsto{\ \operatorname{Fr}\ }
K^a.
\tag{A3.49}
\]

### 12. Boundary realization

The aperture boundary form separates three kinds of boundary data:

\[
q_{\rm bnd}^a
=q_{\rm src-bnd}^a
+q_{\rm face-bnd}^a
+q_{\rm match}^a.
\tag{A3.50}
\]

\(q_{\rm src-bnd}^a\) is inherited from intrinsic source boundary type. \(q_{\rm face-bnd}^a\) imposes the selfadjoint boundary condition appropriate to the aperture face. \(q_{\rm match}^a\) enforces agreement with adjacent faces.

For a differential realization on a manifold with boundary, Green’s identity has the form

\[
\langle D\psi,\phi\rangle-\langle\psi,D\phi\rangle
=\langle\gamma_1\psi,\gamma_0\phi\rangle_{\partial}
-\langle\gamma_0\psi,\gamma_1\phi\rangle_{\partial}.
\tag{A3.51}
\]

Assume the trace maps form an ordinary boundary triple, or a
quasi-boundary triple with the corresponding range and closure
hypotheses, for the maximal realization of \(D\). A face boundary
condition selects a maximal Lagrangian subspace of the boundary
symplectic space:

\[
\mathcal L_{\partial}^a
\subset
\mathcal H_{\partial}\oplus\mathcal H_{\partial},
\qquad
\omega_{\partial}|_{\mathcal L_{\partial}^a}=0.
\tag{A3.52}
\]

Under the boundary-triple hypothesis, maximal Lagrangian subspaces are
in one-to-one correspondence with selfadjoint extensions; (A3.52)
therefore ensures selfadjointness of the realized carrier. Boundary
information is part of the aperture’s operator domain, not an external
correction.

\tag{A3.54}

Here \(K_{\rm reg}^a\) is selfadjoint on

\(C_a^{\rm RD}\in\mathcal B(\mathcal H_{\rm def}^a,

the quadratic-form level rather than by (A3.54).

\mathcal R^a=

Residue is not discarded. It either appears as a coupled carrier channel, becomes an effective nonlocal term after elimination, or prevents the face from producing a Tier-1 field.

### 14. Effective elimination and memory

If \(K_{\rm def}^a+z\) is invertible for \(\Re z>0\), eliminating the defect sector produces the Feshbach–Schur operator

\[
\boxed{
K_{\rm eff}^a(z)
=K_{\rm reg}^a
-C_a^{\rm RD}(K_{\rm def}^a+z)^{-1}(C_a^{\rm RD})^*.
}
\tag{A3.57}
\]

For \(\Re z>0\), (A3.57) is a closed operator on
\(D(K_{\rm reg}^a)\) and is analytic in \(z\). Its causal time-domain
memory kernel is

\[
\mathcal K_{\rm mem}^a(t)
=C_a^{\rm RD}e^{-tK_{\rm def}^a}(C_a^{\rm RD})^*,
\qquad t\ge0.
\tag{A3.57a}
\]

The \(z\)-dependence is the Laplace representation of memory. A local,
constant effective carrier exists only when the defect coupling can be
approximated uniformly on the physical spectral window:

\[
\sup_{z\in\mathcal W_{\rm phys}}
\left\|
C_a^{\rm RD}\bigl[(K_{\rm def}^a+z)^{-1}
-(K_{\rm def}^a+z_0)^{-1}\bigr](C_a^{\rm RD})^*
\right\|
\le\varepsilon_{\rm loc}.
\tag{A3.58}
\]

Without (A3.58), the exact Tier-1 response remains nonlocal in time or scale.

#### 15.1 Coherence-symbol and damping selection

The coherent generator cannot be supplied by an arbitrary function
chosen downstream.  The phase-sensitive relation coordinates of face
\(a\) define continuous affine functionals

\[
\ell_j^a:\mathscr H_a\to\mathbb R,
\qquad
\ell_j^a(h)=\varpi_j^a,
\qquad j\in J_a^{\rm ph},
\tag{A3.64a}
\]

on a real separable Hilbert space of spectral symbols
\(\mathscr H_a\hookrightarrow L^2(\nu^a)\). Elements carry nominated
Borel representatives. If \(0\) is a spectral atom, evaluation at
zero is continuous in \(\mathscr H_a\); intrinsically the pinned-zero
condition means \(h(K^a)E^a(\{0\})=0\). The space is fixed by the face
and includes the conditions

\[
h(0)=0,
